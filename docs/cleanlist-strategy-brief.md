## The market opportunity {#the-market-opportunity .section-title}

comparable app --- Yuka --- reached 76 million global users and \$20M in
annual revenue with zero marketing spend, validating that this category
has strong organic pull. The US market remains underpenetrated relative
to Europe.

## Competitive landscape {#competitive-landscape .section-title}

from \"this product is harmful\" to \"here is a safe alternative
available at your store today.\" That gap is the core differentiation
opportunity.

## Target user {#target-user .section-title}

Three realistic personas were evaluated. The selected primary target ---
the informed health optimizer --- offers the largest addressable market
and the strongest willingness to pay for personalization.

:::::::::::: persona-grid
::::: persona-card
::: persona-name
Persona A --- Anxious new parent
:::

::: persona-desc
28--38, recently had or expecting a baby. Highly motivated, time-poor,
shares tools obsessively within parent networks. High virality, high
LTV.
:::
:::::

::::: {.persona-card .selected}
::: persona-name
Persona B --- Informed health optimizer
:::

::: persona-desc
Health-conscious adult already using wellness apps. Motivated by
personal health outcomes, pays for tools that give granular control.
Largest addressable market.
:::
:::::

::::: persona-card
::: persona-name
Persona C --- Condition-driven shopper
:::

::: persona-desc
Managing a specific diagnosis or sensitivity (eczema, ADHD, hormone
concerns). Highly specific needs, very loyal, active in niche
communities.
:::
:::::
::::::::::::

Personas A and C are strong expansion audiences once the core experience
is proven. Persona C in particular is a valuable early adopter group for
the condition-specific filtering layer of the health profile.

## Strategic risk analysis {#strategic-risk-analysis .section-title}

Three primary risks were identified and worked through. Each risk has a
clear decision and a defined mitigation path.

::::::::: risk-block
::::: risk-header
::: risk-num
1
:::

::: risk-title-text
Data infrastructure
:::
:::::

::::: risk-body
::: decision-pills
[Decisions →]{.pill-label} [Proprietary scoring methodology]{.pill}
[Target as launch retailer]{.pill}
:::

Two separate data problems must be solved: (1) which ingredients are
harmful, and (2) what safe alternatives are available at the user\'s
store. Proprietary scoring --- developed with a credentialed scientific
advisor --- is the right long-term moat. Building on top of transparent
interim sources (EWG, Open Food Facts) while the methodology is
developed maintains user trust at launch. Target offers the most
compelling proof-point for the store-specific alternatives feature: a
large mainstream catalog with high product contrast. Retailer data can
be accessed via Target\'s API partner program or through commercial
aggregators like Datasembly or 1WorldSync.

::: callout
**Watch out:** Proprietary scoring takes 6--12 months to build credibly.
Don\'t launch with claims of independence before the methodology is
documented. Recruit a scientific advisor early --- ideally a
toxicologist or pediatric nutrition specialist.
:::
:::::
:::::::::

::::::::: risk-block
::::: risk-header
::: risk-num
2
:::

::: risk-title-text
Target user definition
:::
:::::

::::: risk-body
::: decision-pills
[Decision →]{.pill-label} [Persona B --- Informed health
optimizer]{.pill}
:::

Persona B is a larger market than new parents, but has lower urgency ---
meaning acquisition needs intent-based channels (search, health
podcasts, wellness newsletters) rather than pure word-of-mouth. The
health profile model is well-matched here: optimizers want granular
control and will pay for a product that learns their specific
sensitivities. The onboarding health profile flow is the make-or-break
activation moment: it must feel meaningfully personalized before the
paywall is encountered. A static questionnaire at signup is insufficient
--- the profile needs to learn and improve with use.

::: callout
**Watch out:** Persona B will abandon apps that feel generic. If the
free tier looks like every other food scanner, they won\'t reach the
paywall. Day 1 personalization is not optional --- it\'s the conversion
driver.
:::
:::::
:::::::::

::::::::: risk-block
::::: risk-header
::: risk-num
3
:::

::: risk-title-text
Business model
:::
:::::

::::: risk-body
::: decision-pills
[Decision →]{.pill-label} [Personalized health profile
(freemium)]{.pill}
:::

The health profile + shopping list builder form a bundled premium tier.
The profile becomes a retention mechanism --- the longer a user uses it,
the more personalized and sticky it becomes, creating genuine switching
costs no barcode-scanner competitor can replicate. Four business model
paths were evaluated: subscription, affiliate/retail, B2B data insights,
and personalized health profile. The profile model aligns strongest with
Persona B\'s motivations. Future expansion paths include B2B aggregate
insights (Year 2--3) once data volume is sufficient, and cautious
affiliate integration only after user trust is established, with full
transparency in ranking methodology.

::: callout
**Watch out:** The health profile only generates switching costs if it
actively improves over time. Build in feedback loops --- e.g. \"did you
find a safe alternative?\" --- that make the profile smarter with each
shopping trip.
:::
